Fashion Revolution Week

Fashion Revolution Week is an annual campaign bringing together the world’s largest fashion activism movement for seven days of action.

About fashion revolution week

Fashion Revolution week happens every year in the week surrounding the 24th of April. This date is the anniversary of the 2013 Rana Plaza collapse.

Rana Plaza, a building in Bangladesh, housed a number of garment factories, employing around 5,000 people. The people in this building were manufacturing clothing for many of the biggest global fashion brands. More than 1,100 people – mostly young women – died in the collapse and another 2,500 were injured, making it the fourth largest industrial disaster in history.

No one should die for fashion.

Fashion Revolution week timeline

The first ever fashion revolution day happened in 2014. In 2016 it expanded to a whole week.

Skip table about fashion revolution week
Fashion revolution day/week effect:
Year Effect
2014 The first fashion revolution day ever, the hashtag #insideout was the no. 1 global trend on Twitter.
2015 63 million people from across 76 countries made the hashtag #WhoMadeMyClothes the number one trend on Twitter.
2016 First year it took place over a week, and 70,000 people around the world asked brands #whomademyclothes with 156 million impressions of the hashtag on social media. More than 1200 fashion brands and retailers that responded with photographs of their workers saying #imadeyourclothes.
2017 There were 533 million impressions on social media posts using one of Fashion Revolution's hashtags during April. Over 2000 brands and producer groups responded, answering #imadeyourclothes.
2018 720 million impressions of posts using one of the hashtags during April 2018, an increase of 35% on last year.
2019 700 million impressions of posts made using fashion revolution hashtags on instagram during April 2019.
2020 Fashion Revolution launched Fashion Open Studio, an international fashion showcase produced digitally to comply with COVID-19 stay at home orders. It also launched a new hashtag #whatsinmyclothes focused on fabric composition and the chemicals used in textile production.
2021 2021 was possibly the biggest Fashion Revolution Week ever, despite the pandemic forcing most of our campaigning online for the second year running, our global community delivered incredible events and activations across the world and made a huge impact.
2022 The theme for Fashion Revolution Week 2022 was Money, Fashion, Power. It builds on the knowledge that the mainstream fashion industry relies upon the exploitation of labour and natural resources.
2023 Next year the Fashion Revolution Week marks 10 years since the Rana Plaza factory collapse.
